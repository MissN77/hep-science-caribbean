About the Author

Brenda Hayles is an award-winning science educator and former Deputy Headteacher with over 25 years of teaching and leadership experience.

She is the author and illustrator of the HEP Science primary curriculum, now used across Haringey, Enfield and Waltham Forest schools and in a network of more than 150 schools nationally.

Brenda designs and leads national CPD programmes and serves as a borough network lead, working with teachers and subject leaders to strengthen curriculum, teaching practice, and leadership.

BETT 2025 presenter - Brenda presented HEP Science at BETT, the world's largest education technology exhibition.

"HEP Science has completely changed how we teach science. Our non-specialist teachers now feel confident delivering every lesson, and the booklets mean every child has the same high-quality resource in front of them. The progression is clear, the vocabulary work is embedded, and the children are genuinely excited about science."

Science Lead, London Primary School

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this digital only?

Yes. Everything is delivered digitally as PowerPoint slides and PDF booklets. You print the student booklets in-house as many times as you need. No shipping costs, no waiting for deliveries, no per-pupil charges.

Do we need specialist science teachers?

No. HEP Science is designed so that any classroom teacher can deliver it confidently. The lesson slides guide the teaching, the booklets structure the learning, and the CPD materials build subject knowledge over time.

Does it align with the Jamaica National Standards Curriculum?

Yes. We've mapped all 36 units against the NSC. You can download the free alignment guide to see the mapping for every year group.

Is training included?

Yes. Every whole-school licence includes an implementation session with Brenda, plus CPD materials your science lead can use with staff throughout the year.

Can we try before we buy?

Absolutely. Email Brenda to request a free sample unit. You'll receive a complete set of lesson slides, a student booklet, and the accompanying vocabulary and assessment materials for one unit.
